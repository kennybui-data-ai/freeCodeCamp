Despite its iconic status, the eye-catching hue was only used for a very short period in McLaren’s history - and it certainly wasn’t Bruce’s (Bruce McLaren) team’s original livery colour.

Here's a brief history of the team colours:

  • 1966 - McLaren's F1 debut in Monaco with a white colour scheme and a central green stripe.
  • 1967 - McLaren switched to a one-off colour scheme of red with a pale central stripe for Canada's inaugural Formula One Grand Prix.
  • 1968 - McLaren made the historic decision to switch to orange. The livery was short-lived, staying only until 1971, but it continues to be a firm favourite with fans.
  • 1972 The arrival of the Yardley sponsor led to a white livery with a small strip of papaya orange on the sidepods.
  • 1974 - Sponsorship deals sparked the iconic red and white blend that would adorn the team's cars for the next two decades.
  • 1986 - McLaren adopted a yellow hue in a one-off livery for Keke Rosberg in Estoril, Portugal.
  • 1996 - Mclaren's last season in the iconic red and white.
  • 1997 - Mclaren tested in a bright orange car. New sponsors sparked the adoption of a silver and black livery - complete with a strip of dayglo red on the underside of the nose.
  • 2006 - McLaren ushered in a revolutionary new chrome livery complete with dayglo stripes running from the front nose to the rear of the car, and also along the top of the engine cover.
  • 2015 - McLaren began the 2015 campaign with chrome still adorning the top of the nose and the sides of the engine cover - but the sidepods were a much darker grey, enlivened only by a dayglo stripe running around the midriff. Five races into the season, McLaren unveiled their darker 'graphite-grey' colouration, with more emphasis on the dayglo keylines.
  • 2017 - McLaren blends old-school McLaren history with contemporary thinking on vehicle livery design, reviving the iconic papaya orange.
  • 2018 - McLaren unveils its updated livery with Papaya Spark along with Burton Blue and Cerulean Blue.

History of McLaren's livery courtesy of Formula 1. If you're interested in learning more about the 2018 design, check out McLaren's official F1 website.
